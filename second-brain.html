Case Study

Personal Second Brain / LLM Wiki

A local LLM-assisted knowledge system built to preserve raw sources, compound useful knowledge over time, and turn one-off chats or notes into structured, reusable information.

Problem

Useful information often gets trapped in one-off chats, screenshots, notes, transcripts, and loosely connected files. That makes it hard to reuse, hard to verify, and easy to lose.

Build

I built a local second-brain system with a clear split between an immutable raw-source layer and an agent-maintained compiled wiki layer. The system uses source summary pages, project pages, concept pages, an index, provenance links, and an append-only operations log so information compounds over time rather than being rediscovered repeatedly.

Why It Matters

This project is one of the strongest examples of my move from support operations into applied AI work. It demonstrates practical grounding, structure, documentation discipline, source handling, and the difference between flashy one-off prompting and durable knowledge engineering.

What It Shows

  • Turning messy source material into reusable knowledge
  • Maintaining provenance and uncertainty rather than overclaiming
  • Thinking in systems, not isolated outputs
  • Using AI as part of an operational workflow rather than as a novelty layer
